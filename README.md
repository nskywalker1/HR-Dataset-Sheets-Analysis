# 🚪 Employee Attrition Analysis Dashboard (HR Insights)

## 📝 Project Overview
This project provides an in-depth **employee attrition analysis** aimed at identifying the key factors that cause employees to leave the organization.

Using a dataset of **1,470 employee records**, the dashboard highlights potential **risk areas across departments, age groups, tenure, and salary levels**.

The main goal of this analysis is to help HR teams **understand turnover patterns and design data-driven retention strategies**.

---

## 📊 Key Metrics
- **Total Employees:** 1,470  
- **Average Tenure:** 7 years  
- **Main Focus:** Attrition drivers across salary, age groups, and tenure

---

## 🔍 Attrition Insights (Why Employees Leave)

### 1️⃣ Early Career Attrition
There is a noticeable spike in attrition among employees aged **25–34**.

This age group typically prioritizes:
- rapid **career growth**
- **skill development**
- better **compensation opportunities**

If these expectations are not met, employees tend to seek opportunities elsewhere.

---

### 2️⃣ Tenure Risk
The data shows that the company struggles to retain employees beyond the **mid-term tenure range**.

With an average tenure of **7 years**, retention strategies may need to focus on **career progression and long-term incentives**.

---

### 3️⃣ Salary Influence
A significant portion of employee turnover occurs among employees earning **less than $5,000 per month**.

This suggests that:
- compensation for **junior and mid-level roles** may not be competitive
- financial dissatisfaction can lead to increased turnover

---

### 4️⃣ Economic Dissatisfaction
The correlation between **lower salary levels and higher attrition** indicates that financial incentives play a major role in employee retention.

This is especially relevant for roles with **higher workload or performance pressure**.

---

## 🧠 Why Do Junior Employees Leave?

Several factors contribute to early-stage turnover:

**1. Financial Motivation**  
Employees often leave after gaining initial experience to secure **higher-paying roles** at other companies.

**2. First Impression & Onboarding**  
High attrition early in employment may indicate issues with:
- onboarding processes
- company culture alignment
- expectation mismatch

**3. Career Stepping Stone**  
Many early-career professionals treat their first job as a **learning stage** before transitioning to roles with higher responsibilities.
